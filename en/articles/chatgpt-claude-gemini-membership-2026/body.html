Decision framework: three questions

Prices and features shift every few months, but the logic for picking a tier doesn't. Ask yourself three things:

  • 1. What's your budget ceiling? $0 → free tier; about $8 → Gemini AI Plus ($7.99, better value than ChatGPT Go); $20 is where most people land and all three compete; $100 is for daily heavy use; $200 is the top tier, worth it only at hours-a-day intensity.
  • 2. What will you mostly do? Coding/agents → Claude (Claude Code) or ChatGPT (Codex); research → all three have Deep Research; images/video → Gemini (Google Flow) or ChatGPT (Sora); everyday Q&A and writing → any $20 tier is plenty.
  • 3. Whose ecosystem are you in? Heavy Google Workspace user → Gemini (it lives inside Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and bundles YouTube Premium); Microsoft 365 user → Claude (M365 and Outlook integrations); neither → fall back to questions 1 and 2.

The $20 battleground: Plus vs Pro vs AI Pro

This is the tier most people pay for, and all three sit at roughly the same price. The difference is emphasis:

  • ChatGPT Plus ($20): the all-rounder. Deep Research, Sora video, Codex, and Agent mode are all included, with the widest model coverage and no ads. Pick it if you want one subscription that does everything.
  • Claude Pro ($20, $17 annual): the cheapest in the tier on an annual plan, and the steady choice for coding and long-form work. Includes Claude Code, unlimited projects, and Research. Pick it if your main job is engineering, writing, or long-document analysis.
  • Google AI Pro ($19.99): the same headline price buys the most extras: YouTube Premium Lite (about $7.99 on its own) plus 5TB storage, with Gemini 3.1 Pro inside Gmail, Docs, and Sheets. If you already live in Google's suite and want ad-free YouTube, it's two things for one bill.

In short: all-rounder → Plus; coding on a budget → Claude Pro; Google ecosystem + YouTube → AI Pro.

The $100 heavy tier

In 2026 all three landed their heavy tier at $100 and went head to head, but the logic differs:

  • Claude Max 5x ($100): simply 5x the Pro usage. For people who hammer Claude Code or long agent runs and keep hitting Pro's ceiling. Same models, more headroom.
  • ChatGPT Pro ($100): a tier OpenAI opened in April 2026, clearly aimed at Claude Max. You only get GPT-5.4 Pro, double context, and 250 Deep Research runs at the $200 step.
  • Gemini AI Ultra (from $99.99): the most bundled: 20x usage, 20TB storage, full YouTube Premium, and high Google Flow credits. For heavy use plus big storage plus YouTube, it's the best value at this price.

Rule of thumb: only climb to $100 when you're hitting limits; only go to $200 when you genuinely need the top models (GPT-5.4 Pro / Max 20x). For a few hours a day, the $20 tier is plenty.
